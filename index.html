20th - 21st March, 2017


About the conclave

It has been more than 25 years since July 1991, when economic liberalisation began life in India. The country witnessed structural changes during the past quarter century. In this context, it is pertinent to discuss the implications of outcomes that have unfolded, and problems that have surfaced for economic development in India.
The Department of Economics at BITS Pilani, K.K. Birla Goa Campus is organizing this event with an intention to stimulate debate and critical thinking under the broad title “Changing Contours of Indian Economy: Welfare Implications & Policy Responses”. The two-day event intends to bring together academicians from institutes of national importance, scholars, practitioners and experts from different disciplines to discuss diverse development policy issues and concerns, namely; Economic Reforms and Manufacturing Sector Growth, Curbing the Black Economy, Role and Significance of MSMEs in India, The Economic Sociology of Fiscal compliance and technological challenges in India, Corporate Financing and Investment Behaviour in India, Measuring Inequality, Poverty and Welfare, Sustainable Development in Emerging Economies with Special Reference to India: Governance Challenges, Towards a Cooperative Commonwealth naming a few.
Thus, this event, certainly, will provide an avenue for disseminating contemporary public policy research and emerging practices with special reference to India across a wide set of domains.
